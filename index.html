Taijiquan has a long history. [1]

Footnote 1: Source reference

This description comes from Cheng Tin Hung’s Tai Chi Transcendent Art (1985).

Push Hands
A partnered Tai Chi exercise for developing sensitivity and balance.
Silk Reeling
Spiraling movement practice in Chen-style Tai Chi.
  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart.
  2. Shift weight onto the right leg.
  3. Step forward with the left foot.

The character 道dào means “way” or “path.”

德(dé) means “virtue.”

Single Whip Movement

In Tai Chi, the movement 單鞭(dān biān) is one of the most iconic postures.

Steps

  1. Shift weight to the right leg.
  2. Open the left arm sideways.
  3. Extend the right hand into a “hook.”

Glossary

Hook Hand
Right hand forming a hook shape with fingers.
Rooting
Stable stance connecting the body to the ground.
Historical note

The form Single Whip appears in early Yang family manuals.
